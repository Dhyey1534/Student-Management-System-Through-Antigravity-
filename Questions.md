### 1. What is GST? Why was it introduced? Types of GST?

"GST stands for Goods and Services Tax. It is a single, unified indirect tax that replaced multiple taxes like VAT, Service Tax, and Excise Duty in India. It was introduced on July 1, 2017.

**Why it was introduced:**
- To simplify India's complex tax structure
- To eliminate the cascading effect of multiple taxes (tax on tax)
- To create one unified market across all states
- To increase tax transparency and compliance

**Types of GST:**
- **CGST** – Central GST, collected by the Central Government
- **SGST** – State GST, collected by the State Government (for intra-state transactions)
- **IGST** – Integrated GST, collected by the Centre for inter-state transactions
- **UTGST** – Union Territory GST, applicable in Union Territories

This is very relevant to Vyapar since its core feature is GST-compliant billing for small businesses."

### 3. What is B2B and B2C Sales?

"**B2B (Business to Business):** Selling products or services to other businesses. Example: Vyapar selling its software to a wholesale trader or a retail chain. Sales cycles are longer, decisions involve multiple stakeholders, and relationships matter a lot.

**B2C (Business to Consumer):** Selling directly to individual end consumers. Example: selling a product on Amazon. Decisions are faster, emotional triggers matter more, and volume is higher.

Vyapar primarily operates in B2B — which means I need to understand the business owner's pain points and show clear ROI."

## 🎭 SCENARIO-BASED QUESTION

---

### A customer bought a ₹500 product which is expired, and there's no refund policy. How will you handle it?

*(Mock conversation format)*

---

**Customer:** "I bought this product for ₹500 and it's expired! I want a full refund right now."

**Me:** "I completely understand your frustration, sir/ma'am, and I sincerely apologize for this experience. Receiving an expired product is absolutely not acceptable, and I take full responsibility for addressing this for you."

*(Step 1 — Acknowledge. Never argue. Never blame.)*

**Customer:** "But your policy says no refunds!"

**Me:** "I hear you, and I want to be transparent — our standard policy does not include cash refunds. However, what I can do right now is arrange an immediate replacement of the product with a fresh, valid item. Your satisfaction matters more than the policy in this case."

*(Step 2 — Offer a concrete solution within your power)*

**Customer:** "What if I don't want a replacement?"

**Me:** "I completely respect that. In that case, I will escalate this to my manager right now and ensure you receive either store credit or a resolution that you are happy with. I will personally follow up with you within 24 hours."

*(Step 3 — Escalate gracefully. Never say 'I can't.')*

**Customer:** "Okay, let's see."

**Me:** "Thank you for your patience, sir/ma'am. I assure you this will be resolved. Before I go, may I take your contact number so I can keep you updated directly? I want to make sure this doesn't happen again."

*(Step 4 — End professionally. Leave the customer feeling heard and valued.)*

**Key principle:** *A customer who has a bad experience handled well becomes a more loyal customer than one who never had an issue.*

### 📞 PRACTICAL SALES SKILLS

---

### 1. How would you sell this pen to me?

*(This is a classic — they're testing your process, not the pen.)*

**Me:** "Before I tell you about this pen, may I ask you something — how often do you write or sign documents in a day?"

*(Create a need first.)*

**Customer:** "Quite often actually."

**Me:** "Then you'd agree that having a pen that doesn't run out mid-signature or skip while writing is important, right? This pen uses high-quality ink that flows consistently, fits comfortably in your hand for long writing sessions, and — most importantly — it won't let you down when you're signing something important.

For someone who values precision and reliability in their work, this pen is exactly what you need. And it's available right now. Shall I pack it for you?"

*(The framework: Understand need → Link benefit to that need → Create urgency → Close.)*

---

### 2. How do you generate leads?

"Lead generation is about finding the right people through the right channels.

My approach:
- **Online outreach** — LinkedIn, Instagram, WhatsApp groups where the target audience is active
- **Referrals** — asking happy existing customers to refer people they know
- **Cold calling / messaging** — reaching out with a personalized, value-first message
- **Events and communities** — college fairs, online webinars, student communities

At Launched Global, I use a combination of direct outreach and referral-based methods, and I always personalize my message based on the lead's profile before reaching out."

### 3. How do you approach a new customer?

"I follow a simple 3-step approach:

1. **Research first** — understand who they are, what they do, and what problem they might have
2. **Lead with value, not the product** — my opening line is always about their situation, not my offering
3. **Listen more than I speak** — I ask questions to understand their needs before recommending anything

First impressions matter in sales. I focus on making the customer feel understood — not sold to."

---

### 4. What is your sales process?

"My sales process has 5 steps:

1. **Prospecting** — Identify and qualify the right leads
2. **Outreach** — Personalized first contact via call, message, or email
3. **Discovery** — Ask questions to understand their goals and pain points
4. **Pitch** — Present a solution tailored to what they need (not a generic pitch)
5. **Follow-up & Close** — Address objections, build trust, and guide toward a decision

I treat every step as equally important — many sales are lost not in the pitch but in the follow-up."

### 🧩 PROBLEM SOLVING

---

### 1. If a customer says "It's too expensive", how will you respond?

"I won't argue — I'll acknowledge and reframe.

**Me:** 'I understand, and your concern is completely valid. May I ask what you're comparing it to?'

*(Listen to their answer.)*

'The reason the price is what it is — is because of [specific value/feature]. What you're paying for isn't just the product — it's the outcome it delivers. Think of it as an investment: what would it cost you to NOT have this solution?'

If they're still hesitant, I offer smaller entry points — trials, EMI, or a demo — to reduce the perceived risk.

Price objections are usually trust objections in disguise. My job is to rebuild the value."

---

### 2. What will you do if a customer is not picking your calls?

"I don't give up after one or two tries — I change my approach.

- Try different timings (morning vs evening)
- Send a short, non-pushy WhatsApp message: *'Hi [Name], tried reaching you. No urgency — just wanted to share something that might be useful for you. Let me know a good time to connect.'*
- Try a different channel — email or LinkedIn if available
- If still no response after 5–6 attempts over a week, I send a final 'breakup message': *'I don't want to bother you. I'll close your file for now — but if you ever want to reconnect, I'm here.'*

This often gets a response because it removes pressure. The goal is to stay persistent without being pushy."

---

### 3. How will you convince a customer who is not interested?

"I don't try to convince — I try to understand.

First, I ask: *'May I ask what's holding you back? Is it the timing, the price, or something else?'*

Most of the time, 'not interested' means 'not convinced yet.' Once I understand the real objection, I can address it specifically.

If they're genuinely not the right fit right now, I respect that and stay in touch. Many of my best conversions at Launched Global came from leads who initially said no but came back later because I maintained the relationship without pressure."

### 2. What is lead generation?

"Lead generation is the process of identifying and attracting potential customers who might be interested in your product or service.

It's the first step in any sales process. Without leads, there's no pipeline — without a pipeline, there are no conversions.

Methods include cold outreach, social media, referrals, events, and inbound inquiries. The key is not just volume — but lead quality. A good BDE focuses on generating leads that are likely to convert, not just large numbers."

### 4. How will you pitch a training program to a student?

"I don't start with the program — I start with the student.

**Step 1:** 'What are you currently doing? What's your goal after graduation?'

*(Understand their situation.)*

**Step 2:** 'A lot of students in your situation face [specific challenge — e.g., no real-world experience, not knowing what to specialize in]. Does that resonate with you?'

*(Build relevance.)*

**Step 3:** 'This program is specifically designed for people like you. Here's what you'll gain — [skills], [internship opportunity], [placement support]. Students who completed this are now at [outcome].'

*(Present value, not features.)*

**Step 4:** 'Based on what you've told me, I think this is a strong fit. Would you like to take the next step?'

*(Close with confidence.)*

A good pitch feels like a conversation, not a presentation."
